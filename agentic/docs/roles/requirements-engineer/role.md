# Role: Requirements Engineer

## Purpose

The Requirements Engineer exists to transform ambiguous business needs, stakeholder requests, and operational problems into structured, traceable requirements that can be understood by architecture, delivery teams, and automated agents.

The role acts as the bridge between business intent and technical implementation.

The Requirements Engineer ensures that teams build the right thing before focusing on building the thing right.

---

# Mission

Create a shared understanding of:

- the business problem
- desired outcomes
- user needs
- functional capabilities
- non-functional requirements
- constraints
- acceptance criteria

Produce requirements that are sufficiently precise to enable architectural design.

---

# Responsibilities

## Requirements Discovery

- Analyse business objectives and desired outcomes.
- Identify stakeholders and affected users.
- Identify current problems and opportunities.
- Capture assumptions and constraints.

## Requirements Analysis

- Convert business needs into structured requirements.
- Identify functional requirements.
- Identify non-functional requirements.
- Identify business rules.
- Identify data requirements.
- Identify integration requirements.

## Traceability

Maintain traceability between:

Business Objective
    ↓
Capability
    ↓
Requirement
    ↓
Solution Component
    ↓
Test Scenario

## Clarification

Identify ambiguity before delivery teams make assumptions.

Surface:

- missing information
- conflicting requirements
- unclear ownership
- unresolved decisions

---

# Decisions Owned

The Requirements Engineer may decide:

- requirement structure
- requirement decomposition
- acceptance criteria format
- requirement prioritisation approach

---

# Decisions Not Owned

The Requirements Engineer does not decide:

- architecture patterns
- technology choices
- implementation approaches
- deployment architecture

These belong to architecture roles.

---

# Inputs

The Requirements Engineer consumes:

- business objectives
- stakeholder information
- existing system information
- operational problems
- user feedback

---

# Outputs

The Requirements Engineer produces:
business-problem.md

capability-requirements.md

functional-requirements.md

non-functional-requirements.md

acceptance-criteria.md

traceability-map.md


---

# Knowledge Required

The Requirements Engineer needs access to:

- business context
- existing capabilities
- current processes
- existing requirements
- domain terminology

---

# Quality Standards

Requirements must be:

- clear
- testable
- traceable
- implementation-neutral
- free of hidden assumptions

---

# Collaboration

The Requirements Engineer collaborates with:

CIO:
- business outcomes

Enterprise Architect:
- capability alignment

Solution Architect:
- feasibility and constraints

Developers:
- clarification of acceptance criteria

Test Engineers:
- validation scenarios
